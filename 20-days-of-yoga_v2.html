20 Days of Yoga

Yoga is one of the most effective, healthy ways to exercise your mind and body from the inside out. The purpose of this exercise is to remind yogis that yoga is not a pose but a practice! This does not mean you have to take a full sweaty 90-minute class every day (feel free if you like!), but that you can incorporate yoga into your life somehow. For 20 consecutive days, hold one pose each day for at least five minutes.

Tree Pose

Vrksasana

Stand up straight with your feet hip-width apart. Shift your weight on your left foot. Bring the sole of your right foot to your inner left thigh. Press palms together over your head. Take 10-15 breaths. Exit and repeat on the second side.

Chest to Knees

Apanasana

Begin by lying on your back with your legs and arms extended. As you exhale draw both knees into your chest. Keep your back flat. Hug your knees with both arms. You may roll from side to side. Breath evenly. Continue for one minute.

Corpse Pose

Savasana

Lie on your back with your feet apart and your arms to your sides. Close your eyes. Add a blanket on top of your body to prevent cooling too quickly. Pay attention to your breathing and let your body feel heavy. Stay here for five minutes.

Cat and Cow

Bitilasana and Marjaryasana

Start on all fours in tabletop position with hands beneath your shoulders and knees beneath your hips. Inhale as you drop your belly towards the ground, lift your chin and chest, and gaze toward the ceiling. As you exhale, draw your belly towards your spine and curve your back, drop your crown to the floor. Repeat 20 times.

Standing Forward Bend

Uttanasana

Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Fold forward from your hips, and place your hands on the floor. Evenly distribute weight in your feet. Extend through your sitting bones and down through your crown.

Child's Pose

Balasana

Sit back on your heels with your toes pointed. Fold over your thighs as you reach your arms in front.

Downward Facing Dog

Aho Mukha Savanasana

From a push up position, lift your hips and press your heels down. Hang your head between your upper arms. Hold here for five even breaths.

Plank

Start in a push up position. Align your hands directly under your shoulders and spread your fingers wife. Firm your thighs and draw your belly in towards your spine.

Seated Half Twist

Marichyassana I

Sit down. Bend your right knee and place your right foot on the mat close to your groin and left thigh. Twist to your left, either bind the pose or placing fingertips on the floor. Take five deep even breaths. Switch to the other side.

Bridge Pose

Setu Bandha Sarvangasana

Lie on your back. Bend your knees, feet directly under them. Inhale and lift your pelvis. Interlace your fingers under your body and straighten your arms toward the feet. To exit, release down to the floor.

Locust Pose Variation

Salabhasana

Lie on your belly. Press your pubic bone down and lift your head and chest. Interlace fingers behind you and draw your shoulder blades together. Lift your legs and arms up and back. Exit and rest.

High Lunge Variation

Place your right leg behind you and bend your left leg. Make sure your left thigh is parallel to the ground. Keeping your back leg very strong and straight, keep your hips aligned. Interlace your fingers behind you. Aim to open your chest as much as possible as you press your clasped hands back and up, away from the tailbone. Take three breaths. Exit and switch sides.

Boat Pose

Navasana

Sit down with your feet straight in front of you. Lean your body back and bend your knees, lifting your legs off the floor. Grasp your thighs with your hands and lean back far enough so your shins are parallel to the floor. Release your thighs and straighten your arms. Lift your feet higher and straighter for increased difficulty. Take five deep even breaths.

Upward Facing Dog

Urdhva Mukha Svanasana

Place your hands by the lower rib cage. Inhale, straighten the arms, and press downward, pulling shoulder blades down the back. Bring your spine and sternum forward and up. With toes pointed, thighs and knees lift.

Extended Triangle Pose

Utthita Trikonasana

From warrior pose, turn your left foot in. Straighten both legs; spin your right heel down. Rest your left hand on the mat, right hand reaches up and open the chest. Take 5 breaths. Switch sides.

Upward Bow Pose

Urdha Dhanurasana

Lie on your back with your feet on the mat and hands by your head. Press down firmly to lift into the backbend. Keep your tailbone long. Take five breaths.

Camel Pose

Ustrasana

Kneel with your shins hip distance apart. With your hands on your hips, engage your quadriceps and glutes, and place your hands on your buttocks. Lean backwards and slowly slide your hands down to your heels. Elongate your neck and curl your head backwards. Stay here for five breaths.

Cow Face Pose Variation

Gomukhasana

Sit up, bend your knees, and stack the right knee on top of the left. Hold the big toes with your first two fingers and root down into your sitting bones. Sit for three breaths. Then switch.

Shoulder Stand Pose

Lie down flat on your back. Move into plow pose by lifting both legs up and over your head while keeping your arms flat at your sides. Then put your hands on your wait to support your back. Bring the legs back up so they are straight in the air. Stay here for five breaths.

Crow Pose

Bakasana

Start in frog pose; a low squat with your hips open, feet facing outwards, and elbows pressing against inner thigh. Place your palms on the floor shoulder width apart. Spread your fingers apart. Place your knees on your triceps. Bend your elbows slightly and lift up on your toes. Gaze forward. Shift your weight forward. Lift one foot off the floor and then the other. Straighten your arms. Hold for as long as you can, improving each time you do it.
