20 Days of Yoga

Yoga is one of the most effective, healthy ways to exercise your mind and body from the inside out. The purpose of this exercise is to remind yogis that yoga is not a pose but a practice! This does not mean you have to take a full sweaty 90-minute class every day (feel free if you like!), but that you can incorporate yoga into your life somehow. For 20 consecutive days, hold one pose each day for at least five minutes.

Tree Pose

Vrksasana

Stand up straight with your feet hip-width apart. Shift your weight on your left foot. Bring the sole of your right foot to your inner left thigh. Press palms together over your head. Take 10-15 breaths. Exit and repeat on the second side.

Chest to Knees

Apanasana

Begin by lying on your back with your legs and arms extended. As you exhale draw both knees into your chest. Keep your back flat. Hug your knees with both arms. You may roll from side to side. Breath evenly. Continue for one minute.

Corpse Pose

Savasana

Lie on your back with your feet apart and your arms to your sides. Close your eyes. Add a blanket on top of your body to prevent cooling too quickly. Pay attention to your breathing and let your body feel heavy. Stay here for five minutes.
